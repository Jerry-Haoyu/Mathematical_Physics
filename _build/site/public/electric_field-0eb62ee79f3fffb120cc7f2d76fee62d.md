# Electric Field 

:::{prf:definition}
An electric field $\mathbf{E}$ is a vector field $\rn{n}\to \rn{n}$, where $\mathbf{E}(\vec x_0)$ is the force experienced by a unit charge(positive test charge) at location $\vec x_0$. 
\begin{equation}
    \mathbf{E}(\vec x_0)=\frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_0} \int \frac{\rho(\vec x)}{\|\vec x_0-\vec x\|^3}(\vec x_0-\vec x) d\vec x
\end{equation}
where $\rho$ gives the charge density at the location. 
:::
