Integral Surface¶

Consider the general quasilinear first order PDE:

a(x,y,u)ux+b(x,y,u)uy=c(x,y,u)a(x,y,u)u_x + b(x,y,u)u_y = c(x,y,u)a(x,y,u)ux​+b(x,y,u)uy​=c(x,y,u)

Therefore the vector field (a,b,c)T(a, b, c)^T(a,b,c)T is perpendicular to (−ux,−uy,1)T(-u_x, -u_y, 1)^T(−ux​,−uy​,1)T thus must lie in the tangent plane of u(x,y)u(x,y)u(x,y). Surfaces that are tangent to a vector space is called integral surfaces, hence to find the solution we want to find the integral surfaces of u(x,y)u(x,y)u(x,y).
